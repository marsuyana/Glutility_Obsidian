
The Gluten Exposure Scanner is designed to operate as a sustainable digital health platform that supports individuals with [[Coeliac disease]] while maintaining accessible core safety features.

The platform combines free public tools with premium services and business partnerships.

---

# Core principles

The monetisation strategy follows three guiding principles:

- essential safety tools should remain accessible
    
- advanced tools may be offered through premium services
    
- commercial partnerships should not compromise food safety information
    

These principles ensure the platform remains trusted by individuals with [[Coeliac disease]].

---

# Free user features

The core scanner functions should remain free in order to maximise adoption and improve public safety.

Free features may include:

- basic product barcode scanning
    
- ingredient interpretation via the [[Ingredient risk engine]]
    
- contamination alerts from the [[Cross-contamination model]]
    
- access to the [[Product database]]
    
- basic restaurant safety information
    
- limited [[Exposure tracking]]
    

Providing these tools freely allows the platform to reach a large user base.

---

# Premium user features

Premium features may provide advanced tools for users who want deeper safety insights.

Possible premium features include:

- advanced exposure analytics from [[Exposure tracking]]
    
- detailed contamination risk explanations
    
- travel safety maps for gluten-free dining
    
- personalised safety alerts
    
- symptom correlation insights
    
- priority access to verified restaurant safety data
    

Premium subscriptions help support the continued development of the platform.

---

# Restaurant partnerships

Restaurants may participate in the platform through voluntary safety programmes.

Possible services include:

- gluten safety education programmes
    
- verified gluten-safe restaurant listings
    
- restaurant safety certification
    
- kitchen contamination risk assessments
    
- training for gluten-safe preparation practices
    

Participating restaurants may receive increased visibility in the [[Restaurant safety database]].

---

# Data partnerships

The platform may collaborate with external organisations to improve food safety data.

Potential partners include:

- food manufacturers
    
- gluten-free certification programmes
    
- food safety organisations
    
- medical research institutions
    

These partnerships may provide additional data for the [[Product database]] and the [[Ingredient risk engine]].

---

# API licensing

The scanner’s knowledge systems may eventually be offered as an application programming interface.

Possible applications include:

- integration with grocery retailers
    
- food delivery platforms
    
- restaurant reservation platforms
    
- health and nutrition applications
    

API licensing allows external services to access the scanner’s risk evaluation systems.

---

# Research collaborations

Aggregated and anonymised exposure data may contribute to research on [[Coeliac disease]].

Examples include:

- exposure patterns across regions
    
- contamination risks in food systems
    
- symptom patterns following gluten exposure
    

Research collaborations must follow strict privacy protections.
