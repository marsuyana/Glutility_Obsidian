
**Symptoms of coeliac disease** arise primarily from [[Malabsorption]] and inflammation of the small intestine caused by immune reactions to [[Gluten]].

The severity and type of symptoms vary widely between individuals.

## Gastrointestinal symptoms

Common digestive symptoms include:

- chronic diarrhea
- abdominal pain
- bloating
- gas
- nausea
- constipation in some cases

## Systemic symptoms

Because the damaged intestine absorbs nutrients poorly, many symptoms occur outside the digestive tract:

- fatigue
- iron deficiency anemia
- weight loss
- bone weakness or osteoporosis
- vitamin deficiencies
- growth problems in children

## Mechanistic pathway

[[Villous atrophy]]  
→ reduced absorptive surface area  
→ [[Malabsorption]]  
→ nutritional deficiencies  
→ [[Symptoms of coeliac disease]]
