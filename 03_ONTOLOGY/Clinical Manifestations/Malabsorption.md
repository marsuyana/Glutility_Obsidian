# Malabsorption

**Malabsorption** refers to the impaired absorption of nutrients from the digestive tract into the bloodstream.

In the small intestine, nutrient absorption depends heavily on the large surface area created by the [[Intestinal villi]].

## Role in coeliac disease

In [[Coeliac disease]], immune-mediated damage leads to [[Villous atrophy]], which reduces the surface area available for nutrient absorption.

As the villi become flattened or destroyed, the intestine becomes less efficient at absorbing essential nutrients.

## Consequences

Malabsorption can lead to deficiencies in:

- iron
- folate
- vitamin B12
- calcium
- vitamin D
- protein
- fats

These deficiencies contribute to many of the systemic manifestations of [[Coeliac disease]].

## Clinical effects

Malabsorption may result in symptoms such as:

- diarrhea
- weight loss
- fatigue
- anemia
- bone weakness
- nutrient deficiencies

## Mechanistic pathway

[[Villous atrophy]]  
→ reduced absorptive surface area  
→ [[Malabsorption]]  
→ systemic symptoms of [[Coeliac disease]]
