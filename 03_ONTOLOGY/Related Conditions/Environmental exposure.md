
[[Environmental exposure]] refers to contact with substances in the external environment that may affect biological systems.

In the context of [[Gluten-related disorders]], environmental exposure primarily occurs through ingestion of foods containing [[Gluten]] or through [[Cross-contamination]] during food preparation.

---

## Dietary exposure

Exposure to gluten typically occurs through consumption of cereals containing gluten proteins.

Primary sources include:

- [[Wheat]]
- [[Barley]]
- [[Rye]]
- [[Triticale]]

These grains contain proteins belonging to the [[Gluten]] family, including [[Gliadin]].

---

## Cross-contamination

Gluten exposure may also occur through cross-contamination during:

- food production
- food processing
- cooking
- shared kitchen environments

Small quantities of gluten may remain on surfaces, utensils, or equipment.

---

## Threshold exposure

Individuals with [[Coeliac disease]] may react to very small amounts of gluten.

International food safety standards often define gluten-free foods as containing less than:

- **20 parts per million (ppm)** of gluten.

However, individual sensitivity may vary.

---

## Biological consequences

Environmental exposure to gluten may trigger:

- [[Immune activation]]
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
- [[Systemic inflammation]]

These responses are central to the development of [[Coeliac disease]].
