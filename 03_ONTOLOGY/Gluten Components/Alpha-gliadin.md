# 33-mer gliadin peptide

The **33-mer gliadin peptide** is one of the most immunogenic fragments derived from [[Alpha-gliadin]] proteins found in [[Gliadins]], a component of [[Gluten]].

This peptide is particularly important in the pathogenesis of [[Coeliac disease]] because it is **highly resistant to digestion** and strongly activates the adaptive immune system in genetically susceptible individuals.

## Key characteristics

• Resistant to gastrointestinal proteases  
• Rich in proline and glutamine  
• Contains multiple overlapping T-cell epitopes  

Because of these properties, the peptide can persist in the intestinal lumen and interact with the immune system.

## Immunological role

The 33-mer peptide contributes to disease development through several mechanisms:

- [[Intestinal permeability]]
- [[Zonulin]] 
- [[HLA-DQ2]]
- [[HLA-DQ8]]
- [[T-cell activation]]
- [[Autoimmune response]]

These processes lead to inflammation and damage to the intestinal villi characteristic of [[Coeliac disease]].
