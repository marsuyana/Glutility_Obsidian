[[Gluten]]

Gliadin are one of the two major protein families that form [[Gluten]].  
They are monomeric storage proteins found primarily in wheat and related cereals and are responsible for the **viscosity and extensibility of dough**.

Gliadins are particularly important in the context of [[Coeliac disease]] because several **immunogenic peptide fragments** derived from gliadin trigger immune reactions in genetically susceptible individuals.

## Types of gliadin

Gliadin are divided into several subclasses:

- [[Alpha-gliadin]]
- [[Beta-gliadin]]
- [[Gamma-gliadin]]
- [[Omega-gliadin]]

Among these, **alpha-gliadin** contains the highly immunogenic [[33-mer gliadin peptide]], which plays a central role in triggering the autoimmune response in coeliac disease.
