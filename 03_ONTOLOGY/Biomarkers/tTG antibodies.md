
Tissue transglutaminase (tTG) antibodies are autoantibodies directed against the enzyme [[Tissue transglutaminase]].

They are the most commonly used serological marker for diagnosing [[Coeliac disease]].

These antibodies are produced as part of the [[IgA immune response]] triggered by exposure to [[Gluten]].

## Associated condition
- [[Coeliac disease]]

## Related immune pathway
- [[IgA immune response]]

## Related biomarkers
- [[EMA antibodies]]
- [[Deamidated gliadin peptide antibodies]]

## Diagnostic role
- [[Coeliac disease screening]]
## Mechanistic pathway


[[Gluten]]
→ [[Gliadins]]
→ [[33-mer gliadin peptide]]
→ modification by [[Tissue transglutaminase]]
→ immune activation
→ production of tTG antibodies
→ diagnosis of [[Coeliac disease]]
