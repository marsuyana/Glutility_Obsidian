## Definition

Exposure refers to the introduction of [[Gluten]] into the body through food consumption, contaminated products, or environmental contact.

For individuals with [[Gluten-related disorders]], even very small amounts of gluten exposure may trigger immune activation and pathological responses.

---

## Gluten-containing grains

The primary sources of dietary [[Gluten]] are cereal grains containing gluten proteins.

- [[Wheat]]
- [[Barley]]
- [[Rye]]
- [[Triticale]]

These grains contain storage proteins that form [[Gluten]], which can trigger immune responses in individuals with [[Coeliac disease]] and other [[Gluten-related disorders]].

---

## Food products

Many common foods contain gluten because they are made from gluten-containing grains.

Examples include:

- [[Bread]]
- [[Pasta]]
- [[Beer]]

## Cross-contamination sources

Gluten exposure may also occur through contamination of foods that are otherwise gluten-free.

Common sources include:

- Shared cooking surfaces
- Shared kitchen utensils
- Airborne flour
- Food preparation environments
- Processing facilities handling multiple grains

This phenomenon is described as [[Cross-contamination]].

Cross-contamination is one of the most common causes of accidental [[Gluten]] exposure in individuals following a [[Gluten-free diet]].

---

## Environmental exposure

In some environments, particles of [[Gluten]] may become airborne.

Examples include:

- Bakeries
- Flour mills
- Food preparation areas using wheat flour

Although ingestion is the primary concern, airborne flour can contribute to accidental ingestion of [[Gluten]].

---

## Biological consequences

When exposure to [[Gluten]] occurs in individuals with [[Coeliac disease]] or other [[Gluten-related disorders]], it may lead to:

- [[Immune activation]]
- [[T-cell activation]]
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
- [[Villous atrophy]]
- [[Malabsorption]]

Repeated exposure can result in persistent intestinal damage and ongoing symptoms.
