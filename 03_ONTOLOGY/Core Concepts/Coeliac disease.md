Coeliac disease is a chronic autoimmune disorder triggered by ingestion of [[Gluten]] in genetically susceptible individuals.

It primarily affects the [[Small intestine]] and leads to damage of the intestinal lining.

## Pathophysiological pathway

The development of [[Coeliac disease]] involves a sequence of molecular and immune events.

Simplified pathway:

[[Gluten]]
→ [[Gliadin]]
→ [[33-mer gliadin peptide]]
→ modification by [[Tissue transglutaminase]]
→ binding to [[HLA-DQ2]] / [[HLA-DQ8]]
→ [[Antigen presentation]]
→ [[T-cell activation]]
→ release of [[Cytokines]]
→ [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
→ [[Villous atrophy]]
→ [[Malabsorption]]
## Immune mechanism

## Intestinal barrier dysfunction

Alterations in the intestinal barrier may contribute to the development of [[Coeliac disease]].

Exposure to [[Gluten]] can stimulate release of [[Zonulin]], a protein that regulates [[Tight junctions]] between intestinal epithelial cells.

Disruption of tight junctions may increase [[Intestinal permeability]], allowing gluten peptides to cross the intestinal barrier and interact with immune cells in the intestinal mucosa.
