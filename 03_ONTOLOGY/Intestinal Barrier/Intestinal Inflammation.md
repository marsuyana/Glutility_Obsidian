# Intestinal inflammation

**Intestinal inflammation** refers to the immune-mediated inflammatory response occurring in the lining of the small intestine.

In [[Coeliac disease]], this inflammation is triggered when gluten-derived peptides such as the [[33-mer gliadin peptide]] are presented to immune cells by molecules like [[HLA-DQ2]] or [[HLA-DQ8]].

Activated T cells release inflammatory cytokines that recruit additional immune cells and amplify the immune response.

## Role in coeliac disease

This immune-driven inflammation damages the intestinal lining and contributes to structural injury of the small intestine.

Persistent inflammation leads to:

- damage to [[Intestinal villi]]
- disruption of nutrient absorption
- intestinal tissue remodeling

## Mechanistic pathway

[[Intestinal permeability]]  
→ gluten peptides cross intestinal barrier  
→ presentation by [[HLA-DQ2]] / [[HLA-DQ8]]  
→ [[T-cell activation]]  
→[[Intestinal  inflammation]]  
→ damage to [[Intestinal villi]]  
→ [[Villous atrophy]]  
→ symptoms of [[Coeliac disease]]
