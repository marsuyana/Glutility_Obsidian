
The gut-brain axis describes the bidirectional communication between the gastrointestinal system and the central nervous system.

This communication involves neural, immune, endocrine, and metabolic signaling pathways.

Changes in the gut environment, immune signaling, and microbiome composition may influence neurological symptoms in individuals reacting to [[Gluten]].

---

## Immune signaling

Inflammatory signals originating in the intestine may influence neurological function.

Relevant processes include:

- [[Immune activation]]
- [[Systemic inflammation]]
- [[Cytokines]]
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]

---

## Neurological effects in gluten-related disorders

Immune activation associated with gluten exposure may contribute to neurological symptoms such as:

- [[Brain fog]]
- [[Peripheral neuropathy]]
- [[Gluten ataxia]]

---

## Interaction with the microbiome

The [[Gut microbiome]] may influence the gut-brain axis through immune signaling, metabolic activity, and microbiome imbalance.

Relevant mechanisms include:

- [[Microbiome imbalance]]
- [[Systemic inflammation]]

---

## Related conditions

- [[Coeliac disease]]
- [[Non-coeliac gluten sensitivity]]
- [[Gluten-related disorders]]

---

## Relevance to Glutility

This concept may help explain why gluten exposure events can produce symptoms beyond the gastrointestinal tract.
