## Konjac

Konjac is a plant-derived ingredient made from the root of *Amorphophallus konjac*. It is commonly used for noodles, rice substitutes, jelly products, and fibre supplements.

## Gluten Status

### Naturally Gluten Free

Plain konjac is naturally gluten free because it is not derived from wheat, barley, or rye.

### Safe Classification

- [[CERTIFIED_FREE]] → If certified gluten free
- [[NO_GLUTEN_DETECTED]] → Plain product with safe ingredients
- [[CROSS_CONTAMINATION_RISK]] → Shared facility / unclear processing
- [[CONTAINS_GLUTEN]] → Added wheat ingredients or unsafe sauce sachets

## Common Product Types

- Shirataki noodles
- Konjac rice
- Konjac jelly
- Glucomannan powder
- Konjac flour

## Risk Factors

Check labels for:

- Wheat starch
- Barley malt
- Flavour sachets
- Soy sauce containing wheat
- Shared manufacturing warnings
- Unclear imported labelling

## User Experience Notes

Texture differs from pasta or rice.

Best preparation:

1. Rinse thoroughly
2. Dry fry in pan
3. Add strong sauce / seasoning

## Vienna Availability

Possible sources:

- Spar / Interspar
- dm
- Müller
- Asian supermarkets
- Organic / health stores
- Online retailers

## Scanner Logic Relevance

Konjac is a useful example of:

- Naturally gluten free base ingredient
- Product-level risk depends on additives
- Need to distinguish raw ingredient vs packaged product
- Good training case for barcode + ingredient engine
