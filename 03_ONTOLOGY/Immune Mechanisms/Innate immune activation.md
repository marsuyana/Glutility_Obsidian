Innate immune activation refers to early immune responses that occur without antigen-specific antibodies or T-cell receptors.

In the context of [[Gluten]] and related compounds, innate immune pathways may contribute to inflammatory reactions in some individuals.

## Possible triggers
• [[Gluten peptides]]
• [[Amylase-trypsin inhibitors]]
• [[FODMAP carbohydrates]]

## Associated conditions
- [[Non-coeliac gluten sensitivity]]

## Potential biological effects
- [[Inflammation]]
- [[Gastrointestinal symptoms]]
- [[Intestinal permeability]]

## Evidence status
The role of innate immune activation in gluten-related disorders is an active area of research and mechanisms may vary between individuals.
