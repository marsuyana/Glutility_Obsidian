
**T-cell activation** is a key step in the immune response where T lymphocytes become activated after recognizing specific antigens presented by antigen-presenting cells.

In coeliac disease, gluten-derived peptides such as the [[33-mer gliadin peptide]] are presented by molecules like [[HLA-DQ2]] or [[HLA-DQ8]] on antigen-presenting cells.

When these complexes are recognized by **CD4+ T cells**, the T cells become activated and begin producing inflammatory cytokines.

## Role in coeliac disease

Activated T cells drive the inflammatory immune response that damages the intestinal lining.

This immune activation contributes to:

• [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
• immune-mediated damage to the intestinal lining

These processes lead to the characteristic intestinal damage seen in [[Coeliac disease]].

## Mechanistic pathway

[[Intestinal permeability]]  
→ gluten peptides cross intestinal barrier  
→ presentation by [[HLA-DQ2]] / [[HLA-DQ8]]  
→ [[T-cell activation]]  
→ inflammatory immune response  
→ autoimmune tissue damage in [[Coeliac disease]]
