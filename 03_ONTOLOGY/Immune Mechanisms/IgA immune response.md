The IgA immune response is the primary autoimmune pathway involved in reactions to [[Gluten]].

Immunoglobulin A (IgA) antibodies are produced in response to gluten peptides in genetically susceptible individuals.

## Associated conditions
- [[Coeliac disease]]
- [[Dermatitis herpetiformis]]
- [[Gluten ataxia]]

## Key biomarkers
- [[tTG antibodies]]
- [[EMA antibodies]]
- [[Deamidated gliadin peptide antibodies]]

## Biological effects
- [[Villous atrophy]]
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
- [[Malabsorption]]

## Genetic factors
- [[HLA-DQ2]]
- [[HLA-DQ8]]
Autoimmune response

An autoimmune response occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks the body's own tissues.

In gluten-related disorders, immune reactions triggered by [[Gluten]] may lead to autoimmune damage in genetically susceptible individuals.
