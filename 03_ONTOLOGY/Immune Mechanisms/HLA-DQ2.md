
**HLA-DQ2** is a variant of a human leukocyte antigen (HLA) molecule that presents peptides to immune cells.

It belongs to the **major histocompatibility complex class II (MHC II)** system and plays a critical role in immune recognition.

## Role in coeliac disease

Gluten-derived peptides such as the [[33-mer gliadin peptide]] can bind strongly to the [[HLA-DQ2]] molecule.

When these peptide–HLA complexes are presented on the surface of antigen-presenting cells, they can activate immune cells, particularly **CD4+ T cells**.

This immune activation contributes to the development of [[Coeliac disease]].

## Genetic susceptibility

Approximately **90–95% of individuals with coeliac disease carry the HLA-DQ2 variant**. A smaller proportion of individuals with coeliac disease carry the related variant [[HLA-DQ8]].

However, many people with HLA-DQ2 never develop the disease, indicating that environmental triggers such as [[Gluten]] exposure are also required.

## Mechanistic pathway

[[Intestinal permeability]]
→ gluten peptides cross intestinal barrier  
→ binding to [[HLA-DQ2]]  
→ [[T-cell activation]]  
→ autoimmune damage in [[Coeliac disease]]
