
The innate immune system is the body's first line of defense against pathogens and foreign substances.

It provides rapid, non-specific immune responses and helps initiate the adaptive immune response.

---

## Role in gluten-related disorders

In gluten-related conditions, components of gluten may activate innate immune responses in the intestinal mucosa.

Certain gluten peptides can stimulate epithelial cells and innate immune cells to release inflammatory signals.

This may contribute to:

- [[Immune activation]]
- [[Cytokine release]]
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]

---

## Interaction with the adaptive immune system

Signals from the innate immune system can influence the activation of the adaptive immune system.

This includes processes such as:

- [[Antigen presentation]]
- [[T-cell activation]]
