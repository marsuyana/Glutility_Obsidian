# Cytokines

Cytokines are small signaling proteins released by immune cells that regulate inflammation and immune responses.

They act as communication molecules between immune cells and help coordinate the body's response to infection, injury, or immune activation.

---

## Role in immune activation

During immune responses, activated immune cells release cytokines that influence the behavior of other immune cells.

In gluten-related disorders, cytokines may be released following:

- [[T-cell activation]]
- [[Antigen presentation]]

---

## Cytokines in coeliac disease

When gluten peptides activate immune cells in the intestinal mucosa, inflammatory cytokines are released.

These cytokines contribute to:

- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
- damage to the intestinal lining
- recruitment of additional immune cells

---

## Contribution to systemic inflammation

Inflammatory cytokines may enter the bloodstream and contribute to [[Systemic inflammation]].

This may affect multiple organs and physiological systems.
