Exposure Tracking records suspected or confirmed exposure to [[Gluten]] and monitors potential health effects in individuals with [[Coeliac disease]].

Because gluten exposure can occur through hidden ingredients or [[Cross-contamination]], tracking real-world events helps users identify risk patterns and improve food safety decisions.

This system works together with the [[Ingredient risk engine]], the [[Cross-contamination model]], and the [[Restaurant safety database]] to evaluate potential exposure events.

---

# Exposure events

An exposure event occurs when a user consumes food that may contain [[Gluten]] or has been subject to [[Cross-contamination]].

Exposure events may include:

- scanning a product flagged as HIGH RISK
    
- eating at a restaurant with contamination risk
    
- consuming a product later suspected to contain gluten
    
- accidental exposure during travel or social events
    

These events can be logged in the platform for analysis.

---

# Exposure timeline

The system records the timeline of potential exposure events.

Example data recorded:

- date and time of exposure
    
- food product or restaurant
    
- ingredient risk classification
    
- contamination risk signals
    
- location of exposure
    

This creates a chronological record of potential gluten exposure.

---

# Symptom tracking

Users may record symptoms that occur after exposure.

Possible symptoms include:

- abdominal pain
    
- diarrhea
    
- fatigue
    
- brain fog
    
- nausea
    
- skin reactions such as [[Dermatitis herpetiformis]]
    

These symptoms may appear hours or days after exposure.

---

# Recovery monitoring

Recovery monitoring helps users understand how long symptoms last after exposure.

Data may include:

- duration of symptoms
    
- symptom severity
    
- recovery timeline
    
- repeated exposure patterns
    

This helps users identify environments or foods that frequently lead to symptoms.

---

# Exposure pattern detection

Over time, the system may identify patterns of risk.

Examples:

- repeated symptoms after visiting certain restaurants
    
- contamination risks linked to particular food categories
    
- frequent exposure in shared kitchen environments
    

These insights help users avoid high-risk environments.

---

# Scanner integration

Exposure tracking is automatically linked to scanner activity.

Possible triggers include:

1. scanning a product flagged by the [[Ingredient risk engine]]
    
2. contamination warnings from the [[Cross-contamination model]]
    
3. restaurant risk alerts from the [[Restaurant safety database]]
    

These signals can automatically create a potential exposure log.

---

# Biological relevance

Exposure to [[Gluten]] activates immune mechanisms in individuals with [[Coeliac disease]].

Key biological mechanisms include:

- [[Gliadin]]
    
- [[IgA immune response]]
    
- [[T-cell activation]]
    
- [[Intestinal Inflammation]]
    
- [[Villous atrophy]]
    

Tracking exposures helps users better understand how these immune responses affect their health.

---

# Role within the platform

Exposure Tracking is the personal health component of the scanner system.

It works together with:

- [[Ingredient risk engine]]
    
- [[Cross-contamination model]]
    
- [[Restaurant safety database]]
    
- [[Product database]]
    

Together these systems form the **Gluten Exposure Scanner platform** used by Glutility.
